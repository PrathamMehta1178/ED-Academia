📚 What is Projectile Motion?

Projectile motion is the motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the acceleration of gravity. The object is called a projectile, and its path is called its trajectory.

🔬 Key Variables

  • Initial Velocity (v₀): The speed and direction at which the projectile is launched.
  • Launch Angle (θ): The angle at which the projectile is launched above the horizontal.
  • Range (R): The horizontal distance the projectile travels.
  • Maximum Height (H): The highest point reached by the projectile.
  • Flight Time (T): The total time the projectile is in the air.

⚡ The Physics Formulas

$$R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}$$

Range Formula:

  • $R$ = Range (horizontal distance)
  • $v_0$ = Initial velocity
  • $\theta$ = Launch angle
  • $g$ = Acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)
$$H = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2(\theta)}{2g}$$

Maximum Height Formula:

  • $H$ = Maximum height
  • $v_0$ = Initial velocity
  • $\theta$ = Launch angle
  • $g$ = Acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²)

🔍 What You'll Observe

  • 45° gives maximum range: For the same initial velocity, 45° provides the longest horizontal distance
  • Complementary angles: 30° and 60° give the same range (for same velocity)
  • Higher velocity = longer range: Doubling velocity quadruples the range
  • Parabolic trajectory: The path always forms a perfect parabola

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Sports: Throwing balls, shooting basketballs, golf shots
  • Military: Artillery shells, missile trajectories
  • Space: Rocket launches and satellite orbits
  • Engineering: Water fountains, fireworks displays
  • Safety: Designing safety zones for explosions
  • Entertainment: Theme park rides and water slides
