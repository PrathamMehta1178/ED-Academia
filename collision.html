üìö What are Elastic Collisions?

An elastic collision is a collision where both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved. The objects bounce off each other without any energy loss to heat, sound, or deformation.

üî¨ Key Variables

  • Mass (m): The mass of each object affects how much momentum it carries and how it responds to collisions.
  • Velocity (v): The speed and direction of each object before and after collision.
  • Momentum (p): The product of mass and velocity (p = mv). Momentum is always conserved in collisions.
  • Kinetic Energy (KE): The energy of motion (KE = ¬Ωmv¬≤). Conserved in elastic collisions.

‚ö° The Physics Formulas

$$m_1v_{1i} + m_2v_{2i} = m_1v_{1f} + m_2v_{2f}$$

Momentum Conservation:

  • $m_1, m_2$ = Masses of the two objects
  • $v_{1i}, v_{2i}$ = Initial velocities
  • $v_{1f}, v_{2f}$ = Final velocities
$$\frac{1}{2}m_1v_{1i}^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_{2i}^2 = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_{1f}^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2v_{2f}^2$$

Kinetic Energy Conservation:

In elastic collisions, kinetic energy is also conserved, meaning no energy is lost to heat or sound.

üîç What You'll Observe

  • Equal masses: When masses are equal, velocities are simply exchanged
  • Heavy vs Light: A heavy object hitting a light one will continue moving, but slower
  • Light vs Heavy: A light object hitting a heavy one will bounce back with nearly the same speed
  • Momentum conservation: Total momentum before = Total momentum after
