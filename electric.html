üìö What are Electric Fields?

An electric field is a region around a charged particle where other charged particles experience a force. Electric field lines show the direction and strength of the electric field at each point in space.

üî¨ Key Variables

  • Charge (q): The amount of electric charge, measured in Coulombs (C). Can be positive or negative.
  • Electric Field (E): The force per unit charge at a point in space, measured in N/C.
  • Electric Potential (V): The electric potential energy per unit charge, measured in Volts (V).
  • Distance (r): The distance from a charge affects the field strength.
  • Coulomb's Constant (k): A fundamental constant in electrostatics.

üîç What You'll Observe

  • Field lines point away from positive charges: Positive charges are sources of electric field
  • Field lines point toward negative charges: Negative charges are sinks of electric field
  • Field lines never cross: Each point has a unique field direction
  • Density shows strength: Closer lines mean stronger fields
